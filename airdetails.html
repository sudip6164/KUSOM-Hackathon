Detailed Information on Air Pollution

Air pollution is a major environmental risk to health. Understanding air pollution levels and trends can help us to mitigate the negative health impacts and improve air quality.

What Contributes to Air Pollution?

Various factors contribute to air pollution, including industrial emissions, vehicle exhaust, and natural sources such as wildfires and volcanic activity. The main pollutants include particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10), nitrogen dioxide (NO2), sulfur dioxide (SO2), carbon monoxide (CO), and ozone (O3).

Health Impacts of Air Pollution

Exposure to air pollution can lead to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases, adverse pregnancy outcomes, and even premature death. Long-term exposure can result in chronic health issues and reduced life expectancy.

Solution for AIR Pollution

Implementing stricter emission controls, promoting cleaner energy sources, and enhancing public awareness are essential steps in reducing air pollution and improving air quality.
