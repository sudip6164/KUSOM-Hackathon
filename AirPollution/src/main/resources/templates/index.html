Why Monitor Pollution?

Monitoring pollution is essential for protecting public health, preserving the environment, ensuring regulatory compliance, and combating climate change. By tracking air and sound quality, authorities can identify pollutants that cause diseases, issue warnings, and implement measures to safeguard public health. It also helps protect ecosystems and biodiversity by detecting harmful contaminants. Regulatory compliance is enforced through monitoring, ensuring industries adhere to environmental standards and preventing illegal discharges.
